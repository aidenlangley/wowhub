How to Google Workspace

https://workspace.google.com/features/

Google Workspace is a cloud based solution, so you have to adjust to not having files on your desktop, and that can be a bit of a hurdle. Instead, just about all of your mahi can be done through the browser, Google Chrome in this case, which is great since your files can follow you just about anywhere you go. Documents aren’t tied to the computer you were using any more. This is more secure, and more convenient.

This will be a quick guide with images and an FAQ (frequently asked questions section) at the very end.

Google Chrome

You are probably already using Google Chrome, but if not, you can download it here.

Once installed, we need to add a new profile to Google Chrome. The reason being that you would usually only have a default profile, which is your personal browser, and we want to add a second profile which will be dedicated to mahi. This separates, otherwise known as sandboxing, your configuration and data, and allows your Google Workspace administrator to control certain settings that are applied to your mahi profile, such as the Managed bookmarks bookmarks folder that we’ll get to later.

The first and probably most important piece of the puzzle is Google Drive. You interact with your files here. You can organise to your hearts content.

When you work inside Google Docs, Sheets, Slides or Forms, your documents will appear in your drive automatically. You can organise them at your leisure.
